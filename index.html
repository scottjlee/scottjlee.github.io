About Me

I'm Scott Lee, a Bay Area native, tea connoisseur, and turtle enthusiast. Currently, I'm a junior studying computer science and statistics at UC Berkeley. In the past, I've had the opportunity to work as part of Rubrik's incredible engineering team on their first Saas product, Office 365. I've also been pretty involved in teaching at Berkeley—this is my fourth semester as a TA for our data science courses. I am advised by Joseph Gonzalez at RISELab, where I study spatial priors on ConvNets.

I'm passionate about designing solutions for complex problems. More specifically, my academic and industry interests span artificial intelligence, machine learning, and computer vision. I'm also deeply interested in the intersection of technology and education, including large-scale course logistics and infrastructure platforms. I fill my free hours by climbing rocks, baking sourdough, drinking matcha, and obsessing over music.
